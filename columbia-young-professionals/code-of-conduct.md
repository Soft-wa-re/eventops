# Code of Conduct

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-04

This Code of Conduct applies to all events, activities, meetups, gatherings, online spaces, and related interactions (collectively, “Events”) organized, hosted, promoted, or facilitated by **10x Software LLC**, doing business as **Columbia Young Professionals**, and its affiliated brands, projects, or community groups (the “Organizer,” “we,” “us,” or “our”).

All participants are expected to follow this Code of Conduct as a condition of participation.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Events are intended to be **welcoming, respectful, and enjoyable** spaces for social connection, community building, and shared experiences.

This Code of Conduct exists to:

- Set clear expectations for behavior
- Promote safety and inclusivity
- Empower organizers to address issues early
- Protect participants, venues, and the broader community

---

## 2. Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Treat others with respect, courtesy, and consideration
- Engage in conversations and activities in good faith
- Respect personal boundaries and consent
- Be mindful of how their words or actions may impact others
- Follow all applicable laws, venue rules, and platform policies
- Drink responsibly if alcohol is present
- Comply with reasonable instructions from organizers or venue staff

---

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are **not permitted** at Events or in related online spaces:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or discrimination of any kind
- Unwanted sexual attention or advances
- Physical violence or threats of violence
- Excessive intoxication or disruptive behavior
- Illegal activity, including drug use or underage drinking
- Damage to venue property or personal property
- Stalking, coercion, or persistent unwanted contact
- Repeated or patterned behavior that creates an unsafe or hostile environment

This list is not exhaustive.

---

## 4. Alcohol and Substances

Some Events may take place at venues where alcohol is available.

Participants are responsible for:

- Monitoring their own consumption
- Making safe and legal choices
- Arranging safe transportation to and from Events

The Organizer does not provide alcohol unless explicitly stated and does not monitor or manage consumption.

---

## 5. Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, you are encouraged to:

- Contact an organizer during the Event, or
- Report concerns by contacting the Organizer at **codeofconduct@eventops.soft-wa.re**

Reports will be handled with discretion to the extent reasonably possible.

---

## 6. Enforcement

The Organizer reserves the right, at its sole discretion, to take appropriate action in response to violations of this Code of Conduct, including but not limited to:

- Verbal warnings
- Removal from an Event without refund
- Temporary or permanent bans from future Events
- Reporting behavior to venue staff or authorities when appropriate

Failure to comply with this Code of Conduct may result in immediate removal.

---

## 7. No Retaliation

Retaliation against individuals who report concerns or participate in an investigation of a Code of Conduct issue is not permitted.

---

## 8. Relationship to Other Policies

This Code of Conduct operates in conjunction with:

- Terms of Participation
- Assumption of Risk and Release of Liability
- Refund & Cancellation Policy

Participation in Events constitutes acceptance of all applicable policies.

---

## 9. Acknowledgment

By registering for, RSVPing to, attending, or participating in an Event, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to abide by this Code of Conduct.
